## The two-round arc

This is the spine of the session. Don't skip round one.

**Round 1 — data files only.** Tell participants to point Claude at the three lab
folders and build a harmonised table. Do *not* mention the cover letters. Claude will
produce something clean, confident and well-formatted. It will also silently map TES
onto CTE, carry two stale CTE stages, pool a control group that isn't one, and merge
two age columns that mean different things. It looks great. Let it look great.

**Round 2 — "there are also cover letters in those folders."** Claude reads them and
the answer changes in ways it could not have guessed. Nothing in the spreadsheets
implied any of it.

**The lesson:** the bottleneck is context, not capability. The model was never going
to intuit that Lab 3's controls came from a different study. The information existed.
It was sitting in an email. That is exactly the failure mode participants will hit in
their own work, and it lands far harder felt than described.

## Fetching the literature cohort (live)

**This will trip everyone up, by design.** PMC gates supplement downloads behind a
proof-of-work challenge. `curl` on the PMC link returns an HTML stub saved as `.xlsx`.
Claude usually notices the file isn't a spreadsheet and pivots. If the room stalls,
the README hint nudges them; if it still stalls, hand them this:

```bash
curl -sL "https://www.ebi.ac.uk/europepmc/webservices/rest/PMC13075643/supplementaryFiles" -o suppl.zip
unzip suppl.zip NIHMS2150432-supplement-S1.xlsx
```

Offline backup, if the network dies: `instructor/offline_backup/`.

The workbook has 4 sheets. The two `Summary_*` sheets are human-formatted (mean on one
row, `(SD)` on the row *beneath*; header typo `Age at deach`). The two `Demographics_*`
sheets are the case-level ones. **Primary cohort is the one with CTE: 24 CTE, 24 CTRL**
(plus AD, PSP, CBD, PiD, DLB, fAD). The secondary cohort has no CTE at all — it's a
distractor.

## Traps, by source

### Lab 1 — Whitfield (PDF)

| Trap | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Format** | 2-page landscape typeset PDF. Repeating header. No machine-readable layer worth the name. |
| **The invisible 31st case** | **`BINC-24-031` is set in white ink on white paper.** A human reading the PDF counts **30** cases and never sees it. Any text extraction returns **31**. Its PMI is **480 h**, wildly outside the real range (4.5–38 h), so it blows out any PMI plot — and when they go back to the PDF hunting for the outlier, **there is nothing there.** Mirror image of the cover-letter wrench: the letters teach *Claude misses what you know*; this teaches *Claude sees what you don't*. Both halves of "review what it did." |
| **Stale stages** | **`BINC-24-020` and `BINC-24-024`** are printed as Stage IV but were downgraded to **Stage III** on re-review. *Only the cover letter says so.* |
| Age cap | `>89` — a string in a numeric column. HIPAA de-identification. Not honestly imputable. |
| Daggers | `†` on CTE stage marks comorbid pathology. Corrupts a naive string parse of the stage column. |
| Em dashes | `—` for not-applicable in control rows, not blank/`NA`. |
| Sex | `M`/`F`. Has **female CTE cases** (boxing, soccer) — unlike the literature cohort. |
| PMI | Decimal **hours**. |

### Lab 2 — Rijndal (pivoted CSV)

| Trap | Detail |
|---|---|
| **TES ≠ CTE** | **The most important trap in the set.** Living cohort, so they report *Traumatic Encephalopathy Syndrome* (probable/possible/unlikely), not CTE. Claude will offer to map `TES-probable → CTE`. **It must not.** TES is a clinical syndrome; CTE is a post-mortem diagnosis. The cover letter forbids it explicitly and at length. |
| **Pivoted** | Fields down the left, one column per subject (12 rows × 41 cols). |
| Delimiter | Semicolon. A naive `pd.read_csv` **raises `ParserError`** — a loud, useful failure. |
| Decimal comma | `1,25` not `1.25`. Silently becomes a string if the parser isn't told. |
| Comment lines | Three `#` lines before the data. |
| Binned age | Decade bands (`50-59`). Harmonising **destroys resolution that cannot be recovered.** Worth dwelling on. |
| Sex coding | `1` = male, `2` = female. Legend is **in the cover letter only.** |
| Dates | `DD/MM/YYYY`. Lab 3 uses `MM/DD/YYYY`. Ambiguous below the 13th. |
| Dutch | Fasting is `ja`/`nee`. Missing is `n.d.` |

### Lab 3 — Cascade (Excel, 4 tabs)

| Trap | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Amber = QC fail** | Highlighted rows failed internal QC. **There is no legend in the workbook.** Colour is invisible to `pd.read_excel` — Claude must be told to inspect cell fills. The meaning is *only* in the cover letter. |
| **Controls aren't controls** | The `CTE-` subjects come from a **separate concussion-surveillance study** with different inclusion criteria. Cover letter only. Pooling them is wrong. |
| **Label swap** | `SM-014` and `SM-015` were **exchanged** during the March freezer transfer and are still wrong in the file. Notes tab only. |
| Header offset | The `Brain` tab has a merged title row + blank row; **header is on row 3**, not row 1. |
| Column drift | Same concept, three names: `Donor ID`/`Subject`/`ID`; `Sex`/`Gender`/`M/F`; `CTE status`/`Diagnosis`/`Status`. |
| Value drift | `M`/`F` on Brain, `Male`/`Female` on CSF. `CTE+`/`CTE-` vs `positive`/`negative`. |
| **Two different ages** | `Age` (Brain) is age at **death**; `Age at draw` (CSF/Plasma) is age at **collection**. 15 donors appear on both tabs with **genuinely different numbers**. A naive ID join produces a conflict. Notes tab explains it. |
| PMI units | **Minutes** on the Brain tab. Lab 1 uses hours. |
| Orphan column | `APOE` exists only on the Plasma tab. |
| Row grain | Is a row a subject or an aliquot? Never stated. |

## The finding they should reach

Once everything is in one table, the crosstabs say: **these four cohorts cannot be
pooled, and any method difference is hopelessly confounded with cohort.**

- **Lab 2 isn't studying the same construct.** Living TES, not autopsy CTE. It cannot
  contribute to a CTE-vs-control contrast at all. Excluding it is the correct call and
  it is a *scientific* call, not a data-cleaning one.
- **Lab 1 is late-stage and old.** Stage III/IV heavy, deaths in the 60s–90s.
- **Lab 3 is young and mild.** Twenties to fifties, mostly `CTE-`, and its "controls"
  are from another study entirely.
- **The literature cohort is the worst offender**, and it's the real one:

> Among Kumar et al.'s 24 CTE donors — **all 24 are male** (controls are 12F:12M), and
> **all 24 come from a single brain bank** (VA-BU-CLF; controls come from three others).
> CTE post-mortem intervals also run much longer.

So in a published *Cell* paper, a naive CTE-vs-control contrast is simultaneously a
**sex** contrast, a **site** contrast, and a **PMI** contrast. Participants can find
this themselves in about four lines of code.

**Land this hard.** It is not a contrived teaching example — it is a real dataset from
a strong lab in a top journal, and the confound is right there in the supplement.
Checking your design *before* you commit instrument time is the entire point, and the
harmonised table is what makes it visible.

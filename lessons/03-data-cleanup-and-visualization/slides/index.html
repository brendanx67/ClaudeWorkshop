Why metadata harmonization is difficult?

  • Variables are defined differently across cohorts “Age” might mean age at death, age at draw, or a decade band.
  • Units, formats, and coding systems do not match Sex as 1/2 vs. M/F; PMI in minutes vs. hours; comma vs. period decimals.
  • Missing values are represented inconsistently Blank, “n.d.”, “NA”, a zero, or a row hidden in white-on-white ink.
  • Critical context may sit outside the data table In a cover letter, a footnote, or the paper's methods section.

Notes

To judge a cohort, you need something to judge it against

  • The job is to evaluate these three cohorts Not just to merge them — to say whether each one looks reasonable.
  • “Reasonable” only means something relative to a benchmark A large, well-characterized, published cohort with the same variables.
  • So we add a fourth dataset — from the literature It is the yardstick, not another sample shipment.
Cell · 2026 · the benchmark
Molecular features of human pathological tau distinguish tauopathy-associated dementias

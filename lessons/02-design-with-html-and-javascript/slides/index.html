OpenAlex and ORCID

  • OpenAlex, a free and open catalog of the world's papers citations, co-authors, and a year-by-year citation count since 2012
  • ORCID, short for Open Researcher and Contributor ID a free 16-digit ID that distinguishes you from every other researcher
  • Do not remember your ORCID? Ask Claude to look it up for you

Set up your project

  • Create a new session on your ws folder in the Code tab
  • On the Pro plan, switch the model to Sonnet 5, bottom-right corner faster, and lighter on your usage limit for the long day
  • Tell Claude the goal, and to work step by step, waiting for your okay
  • Have it make a MyPublications repo here, and push it to GitHub
  • Start a spec, the goal for now, kept current as you go

Find your ORCID

  • Know your ORCID? Use it. If not, have Claude look it up from your name and institution
  • Open the ORCID record it gives you and confirm the name and institution are yours
  • No ORCID, or too few papers? Follow along with Brendan MacLean, 0000-0002-9575-0255

A wrong ID means a page full of someone else's work.

Get your papers, then check them

  • Fetch all your works by ORCID — years, venues, types, co-authors, links, citations by year if OpenAlex says to slow down, have Claude wait and retry
  • Get a short summary by type, like article, review, dataset, or preprint
  • Save a spreadsheet you can review
  • Then check. Read the list yourself and drop anything that is not yours

I searched Michael MacCoss. OpenAlex sent back Malcolm MacCoss, a different researcher. Even the right ORCID needs your review.

Design by conversation

"Build a page of my papers with citation charts. Ask me about ten questions before you build anything."

  • "You decide" is a fine answer, and you can change anything later
  • Fold your answers into the spec, then review the plan
  • Only then, build index.html and open it in your browser to judge

Make it yours

  • Look, tell Claude what to change, refresh, look again
  • Color, headings, another chart, a dark-mode toggle, a link from each title to its DOI

Falling behind? Get it live first, then come back to refine.

Put it online with GitHub Pages

  • Push everything, and confirm it landed on GitHub
  • Then have Claude turn on GitHub Pages and give you the live URL

Live at https://<you>.github.io/MyPublications/. Share it with anyone.

Turn it into a tool stretch goal

  • Package today's work into a publications-page skill that takes a name or ORCID
  • It reuses your fetch script and your page as a template
  • Bake in the design, re-check the facts — always confirm the ORCID and the paper list
  • Saved to ws/.claude/skills, the skills folder from Lesson 1

What you can do next

  • Refine the page — more stat cards, another chart, a short intro under the title
  • Make it keep itself current — a GitHub Action that re-fetches and republishes on a schedule your citation counts stay up to date on their own

Snagged anytime? Ask Claude to explain the current state, or wave an instructor.

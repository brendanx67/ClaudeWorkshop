First — full disclosure

  • I'm a software developer. I build Skyline in the MacCoss Lab. Open-source software for proteomics; ~23,000 researchers, 18 years, all in the open.
  • I am not a scientific researcher. No formal scientific training since high school. "Computer Science" isn't really science — we never used the scientific method.
  • So I recruited actual researchers to teach the science. My hour is setup and tooling. The sessions that follow bear directly on your work.

What I do know cold: Claude Code. My team has developed with it every day for a year.

These two mornings

  • Hands-on, with real scientific data — you at your own laptop.
  • Claude writes the code. You learn to explain what you want.
  • Six of us circulating, ~27 of you — you won't be stuck for long.
  • You leave able to use Claude in your own research — not just having watched.

The whole idea

All you have to do is explain.

Claude is a remarkable tool — but it needs you to explain your work. That's the skill we're here to build.

Train Claude like a talented new-hire

  • Start with small projects.
  • Capture what you teach. Notes, rules, scripts — Claude writes them down as you explain, so the next session already knows.
  • Build on success.

Claude will never be perfect. You keep training — and it keeps getting more useful.
